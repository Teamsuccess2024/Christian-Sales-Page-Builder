**Plug-and-Play Sales Page Structure (All Ten Methods)**

**How to Use This Skeleton**

This template is the writing backbone for any Christian sales page Claude drafts. Every section below contains:

- **Section name and purpose**
- **Method(s) deployed** (cross-referenced to the ten methodologies)
- **Length target**
- **Plug-and-play structure** with \[BRACKETED VARIABLES\] for offer-specific inputs
- **Inline guidance** showing how to write each part

Variables in \[BRACKETS\] are filled in from intake answers. Claude replaces every bracket before delivering the page. If a bracket can't be filled because the intake didn't cover it, Claude flags it for the user rather than inventing content.

This skeleton produces a mid-ticket sales page (\$77-\$197) by default. For price tier adjustments, see the notes at the bottom.

**SECTION 1 - HERO (ABOVE THE FOLD)**

**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. Promise specific transformation. Earn the next five seconds. **Methods:** 2 (Scripture-Anchored Big Idea), 4 (Transformation Bridge - opening half) **Length:** One screen on desktop, scannable in 5 seconds.

**\[OPTIONAL PRE-HEADLINE - 4-7 words identifying the reader\]**

_Example structure: "For the \[READER IDENTITY\] walking through \[SPECIFIC SITUATION\]..."_

**\[MAIN HEADLINE - the page's biggest promise, anchored in the Big Idea\]**

_Headline formula options (Claude picks one based on intake):_

- _Identity + Promise: "Your \[PAIN/SITUATION\] Isn't \[WRONG ASSUMPTION\] - It's \[BIG IDEA REFRAME\]"_
- _Question hook: "What If \[PAIN\] Was Actually \[BIG IDEA REFRAME\]?"_
- _Scripture-anchored: "\[CORE SCRIPTURE PARAPHRASE\] - And How \[N\] Books Will Walk You Through It"_
- _Specificity-driven: "The \[N\]-Book Journey for Christians Walking Through \[SPECIFIC PAIN\]"_

**\[SUB-HEADLINE - one sentence specifying the transformation and timeframe\]**

_Structure: "\[CONCRETE BEFORE STATE\] → \[CONCRETE AFTER STATE\] in \[TIMEFRAME\] through \[METHOD\]."_

\[VISUAL: Stacked book covers / product mockup / author photo - placed prominently\]

**\[PRIMARY CTA BUTTON - action verb + outcome, not "Buy Now"\]**

_CTA examples: "Start the Journey for \$\[PRICE\]" / "Get All \[N\] Books - \$\[PRICE\]" / "Begin Today - \$\[PRICE\]"_

_\[Optional trust line under button: "30-day guarantee. Instant access. \[N\] readers and counting."\]_

**SECTION 2 - EMPATHY / STORY OPENING**

**Purpose:** Prove you understand the reader's pain before pitching anything. **Methods:** 1 (Testimony-Driven PAS - Problem + Agitate phases), 7 (StoryBrand - hero meets pain) **Length:** 200-400 words.

\[OPENING HOOK SENTENCE - names the exact feeling the reader carries right now\]

_Example structure: "You've \[SPECIFIC ACTION reader has taken repeatedly\] and \[SPECIFIC OUTCOME they keep getting\]."_

\[PARAGRAPH 1 - expand the feeling with 3-4 specific details that signal "I've been here."\]

_Pull from intake answers 3.2, 4.5, 4.6 - use the reader's own language, not marketing language._

\[PARAGRAPH 2 - author testimony OR representative reader story\]

_If author has personal testimony for this pain, lead with it:_ _"I know this because I \[AUTHOR'S OWN STORY WITH THIS PAIN\]."_

_If author doesn't have direct testimony, use representative story:_ _"I think of \[READER ARCHETYPE\] who \[SPECIFIC SITUATION\] - and how their story is more common than the church admits."_

\[PARAGRAPH 3 - the turning point that creates curiosity\]

_Structure: "Then \[SOMETHING SHIFTED\]. And it wasn't \[OBVIOUS EXPECTATION\]. It was \[BIG IDEA TEASE\]."_

\[TRANSITION SENTENCE INTO SECTION 3\]

_Example: "But before I show you what changed everything, you need to see what staying here costs you."_

**SECTION 3 - THE PROBLEM DEEPENED**

**Purpose:** Show the real cost of staying where the reader is - not to manipulate, to clarify. **Methods:** 1 (Testimony-Driven PAS - Agitate phase), 8 (Awareness-Stage Layering) **Length:** 150-300 words.

**\[SUBHEAD - names the hidden cost\]**

_Example structures:_

- _"What \[PAIN\] Does to a Believer Over Time"_
- _"The Real Cost of Walking Through This Alone"_
- _"Why \[PAIN\] Without \[SOLUTION\] Quietly Reshapes Your Faith"_

\[PARAGRAPH 1 - the spiritual cost of unaddressed pain\]

_Name what happens to faith when suffering has no biblical framework. Specific, not abstract._

\[PARAGRAPH 2 - the relational and emotional cost\]

_Isolation in pain compounds it. Name how the reader's relationships, identity, or daily life are quietly being reshaped._

\[PARAGRAPH 3 - one anchor scripture validating the experience\]

_"\[SCRIPTURE VALIDATING THE READER'S PAIN\]"_ - \[REFERENCE\]

\[ONE-SENTENCE APPLICATION OF THE VERSE - connects scripture to the reader, no more.\]

\[TRANSITION SENTENCE INTO SECTION 4\]

_Example: "But here's what most Christians never hear about \[PAIN\] - and what changes everything once you see it."_

**SECTION 4 - THE REFRAME / BIG IDEA REVEAL**

**Purpose:** Drop the contrarian biblical truth that reframes the reader's entire situation. This is the page's pivot point. **Methods:** 2 (Scripture-Anchored Big Idea - full deployment), 3 (Belief Breaking - first false belief) **Length:** 200-350 words.

**\[SUBHEAD - the Big Idea stated boldly\]**

_Example structures:_

- _"Your \[PAIN\] Isn't \[ASSUMED MEANING\] - It's \[BIG IDEA REFRAME\]"_
- _"\[BIBLICAL FIGURE\] Knew Something About \[PAIN\] That Most Christians Miss"_
- _"The Verse That Reframes Everything: \[SCRIPTURE PARAPHRASE\]"_

\[PARAGRAPH 1 - state the Big Idea clearly and confidently\]

_The Big Idea in one bold sentence. No hedging. No "perhaps" or "maybe."_

\[PARAGRAPH 2 - anchor scripture or biblical pattern\]

_"\[ANCHOR SCRIPTURE\]"_ - \[REFERENCE\]

\[ONE OR TWO SENTENCES explaining why this verse / this biblical figure reframes the situation\]

\[PARAGRAPH 3 - application of the Big Idea to the reader's life\]

_Structure: "Which means \[PAIN\] isn't \[WRONG ASSUMPTION\]. It's \[REFRAME\]. And once you see that, you can't unsee it."_

\[BRIDGE SENTENCE INTO SECTION 5\]

_Example: "But seeing the reframe is one thing. Walking it out is another - and that's where most Christians get stuck. Here's why."_

**SECTION 5 - BELIEF BREAKING (THE THREE WALLS)**

**Purpose:** Dismantle the specific false beliefs blocking the reader from saying yes. **Methods:** 3 (Belief Breaking - full deployment) **Length:** 300-500 words. One paragraph per belief.

**\[SUBHEAD - names the three walls\]**

_Example: "Three Lies Christians Believe About \[PAIN\] - And Why They Keep You Stuck"_

**Wall 1 - The Vehicle Belief**

\[STATE THE BELIEF as the reader holds it\]

_Example: "Books can't actually help with real pain. They're just words."_

\[REBUTTAL - short, scripture-anchored or story-anchored\]

_Structure: "But \[COUNTER-EXAMPLE / SCRIPTURE / STORY\]. The right book, read in the right season, with the right framework, does something different than reading for information - it \[SPECIFIC OUTCOME\]."_

**Wall 2 - The Internal Belief**

\[STATE THE BELIEF\]

_Example: "I don't have the faith to walk through this well. Other Christians might, but not me."_

\[REBUTTAL\]

_Structure: "But \[SCRIPTURE / TRUTH about how faith is built, not summoned\]. The believers who walked through \[PAIN\] well weren't stronger than you - they had \[SPECIFIC RESOURCE / FRAMEWORK\]. This bundle is that resource."_

**Wall 3 - The External Belief**

\[STATE THE BELIEF\]

_Example: "My situation is too specific / too far gone / too unique for any general resource to help."_

\[REBUTTAL\]

_Structure: "But \[SCRIPTURE about the universality of suffering / God's intimacy with specifics\]. \[BIBLICAL FIGURE\]'s situation was specific too. So was \[SECOND FIGURE\]'s. The pattern doesn't change because your circumstance is unique."_

\[BRIDGE SENTENCE INTO SECTION 6\]

_Example: "Once those three walls come down, the path forward becomes clear. Here's what it looks like."_

**SECTION 6 - THE OFFER REVEAL**

**Purpose:** Introduce what the reader is being invited into. Name it, frame it, position it. **Methods:** 4 (Transformation Bridge - bridge revealed), 5 (Curated Library Frame, if applicable) **Length:** 100-200 words.

**Introducing \[OFFER NAME\]**

\[ONE-SENTENCE POSITIONING LINE\]

_Structure: "The \[N\]-\[FORMAT\] \[JOURNEY/SYSTEM/PATH\] for Christians \[SPECIFIC SITUATION\] who want \[SPECIFIC OUTCOME\]."_

_Example: "The 10-book journey for Christians walking through suffering they didn't choose - who want scripture-anchored companionship instead of platitudes."_

\[VISUAL: Full bundle stack / product mockup / clear image of what they get - placed here, prominently\]

\[PARAGRAPH - 2-4 sentences positioning what makes this different\]

_Pull from intake answers about format, curation, what's curated and why, what's different from free content elsewhere if applicable._

\[BRIDGE SENTENCE INTO SECTION 7\]

_Example: "Here's exactly what's inside, what each book addresses, and the biblical foundation under each one."_

**SECTION 7 - THE VALUE STACK**

**Purpose:** Build perceived value section by section. By price reveal, value should obviously exceed cost. **Methods:** 6 (Value Stack with Stewardship Framing - full deployment), 5 (Curated Library Frame - for library offers) **Length:** 600-1,200 words. The page's longest section by design.

**What You Get When You Join \[OFFER NAME\]**

**\[COMPONENT 1 NAME\]**

\[VISUAL - book cover / module thumbnail\]

**Addresses:** \[SPECIFIC PAIN OR QUESTION THIS COMPONENT MEETS\]

**What's inside:** \[1-2 sentence description of what the reader actually gets - chapters, length, structure\]

**Anchored in:** \[ANCHOR SCRIPTURE OR BIBLICAL PRINCIPLE\]

**Value:** \$\[DOLLAR VALUE\]

**\[COMPONENT 2 NAME\]**

\[VISUAL\]

**Addresses:** \[SPECIFIC PAIN OR QUESTION\]

**What's inside:** \[DESCRIPTION\]

**Anchored in:** \[SCRIPTURE\]

**Value:** \$\[DOLLAR VALUE\]

_\[Repeat the above pattern for every component in the offer. For a 10-book bundle, you'll have 10 of these blocks.\]_

**\[BONUS 1 NAME\] _(if applicable)_**

\[VISUAL\]

**Why it's included:** \[WHAT THIS BONUS DOES FOR THE BUYER\]

**Value:** \$\[DOLLAR VALUE\]

**\[BONUS 2 NAME\] _(if applicable)_**

\[Same structure as Bonus 1\]

**Total Value: \$\[STACKED TOTAL\]**

\[TRANSITION PARAGRAPH - frame the stack as serious value before revealing price\]

_Structure: "If you bought \[N\] Christian books from a \[BOOKSTORE/CONFERENCE\], you'd spend \$\[REALISTIC COMPARISON\]. If you sat with a Christian counselor for the \[N\] hours it would take to walk this content, you'd spend \$\[COMPARISON\]. The total value above reflects that honestly."_

\[BRIDGE INTO SECTION 8 - social proof\]

_Example: "Before I show you the price, here's what other Christians who've walked this path have said."_

**SECTION 8 - SOCIAL PROOF**

**Purpose:** Show the reader they're not alone in saying yes, and that real people have walked this with real results. **Methods:** 4 (Transformation Bridge - proof of after-state), 7 (StoryBrand - proof other heroes succeeded) **Length:** 300-600 words.

**What Christians Are Saying**

\[TESTIMONIAL 1\]

_"\[SPECIFIC TESTIMONIAL - names a particular transformation, not generic praise\]"_ - **\[NAME\], \[LOCATION OR CONTEXT\]**

\[TESTIMONIAL 2\]

_"\[SPECIFIC TESTIMONIAL\]"_ - **\[NAME\], \[LOCATION OR CONTEXT\]**

\[TESTIMONIAL 3\]

_"\[SPECIFIC TESTIMONIAL\]"_ - **\[NAME\], \[LOCATION OR CONTEXT\]**

_\[Continue with 3-7 testimonials for mid-ticket, 7-15 for high-ticket. If real testimonials don't exist yet, use testimonials about the author's previous work, the source material, or methodology - labeled honestly.\]_

_\[Optional: Include 1-2 testimonials with photos if available - these convert significantly higher.\]_

\[BRIDGE INTO SECTION 9 - pricing\]

_Example: "When you see what's inside and what other Christians have walked through with this material, the price question becomes simpler. Here's what it costs."_

**SECTION 9 - THE PRICE REVEAL AND STEWARDSHIP FRAMING**

**Purpose:** Present price as stewardship, not expense. **Methods:** 6 (Value Stack close), 10 (Risk Reversal - soft urgency introduced) **Length:** 150-300 words.

**Your Investment**

\[RESTATE STACKED VALUE\]

_Example: "Total value if purchased separately: \$\[STACKED TOTAL\]"_

\[REVEAL THE ACTUAL PRICE\]

_Example: "Your price today:_ **_\$\[PRICE\]_**_"_

\[STEWARDSHIP FRAMING LINE\]

_Pick one based on price tier (see references/pricing-positioning.md):_

- _Low-ticket: "Less than a coffee and a muffin - but this stays with you."_
- _Mid-bundle: "Less than \$\[PER-UNIT MATH\] per book - and you'd pay \$15-20 each at a Christian bookstore."_
- _Premium: "Less than \$25 per month over the next year - and you keep it forever."_

_\[Optional, only when appropriate - tithe-equivalency framing:\]_ _"For many buyers, this is roughly \[ONE WEEK / ONE MONTH\] of normal giving - directed toward your own spiritual library."_

\[PAYMENT PLAN OPTION, IF OFFERED\]

_Example: "Or 3 payments of \$\[AMOUNT\], billed monthly."_

**\[CTA BUTTON - second placement on page\]**

_Same CTA copy as Section 1 button, for consistency._

\[BRIDGE INTO SECTION 10 - guarantee\]

_Example: "And if it doesn't meet you where you are, here's what happens."_

**SECTION 10 - THE GUARANTEE**

**Purpose:** Remove the risk of saying yes. Make the decision feel safe. **Methods:** 10 (Risk Reversal - full deployment) **Length:** 100-200 words.

**The \[N\]-Day "Meet Me Where You Are" Guarantee**

\[OPENING SENTENCE - frame the guarantee pastorally, not legally\]

_Example: "If you go through this bundle and it doesn't meet you where you are, I don't want your money."_

\[PARAGRAPH - state the guarantee plainly\]

_Structure: "Try \[OFFER NAME\] for \[N\] days. Read \[SPECIFIC AMOUNT\]. If it doesn't \[SPECIFIC OUTCOME\], email me at \[EMAIL\] and I'll refund every dollar. No questions, no hoops, no guilt."_

\[CLOSING REASONING LINE\]

_Example: "Christians have been burned by enough marketing. This guarantee is my way of saying: I'd rather lose the sale than ask you to take a risk on something that doesn't serve you."_

\[OPTIONAL: Guarantee badge or seal visual\]

**Questions Other Christians Have Asked**

**\[OBJECTION 2 - typically about reading capacity or time\]**

_Example: "What if I'm not a strong reader / don't have much time?"_

\[ANSWER\]

**\[OBJECTION 3 - typically theological\]**

_Example: "Is this prosperity gospel? Is this charismatic? What's the theological positioning?"_

\[ANSWER - direct and honest about the author's theological lane\]

**\[OBJECTION 4 - typically denominational\]**

_Example: "What denominations is this for?"_

\[ANSWER\]

**\[OBJECTION 5 - typically about fit for the specific situation\]**

_Example: "What if my pain is \[SPECIFIC SITUATION not addressed in the offer description\]?"_

\[ANSWER\]

**\[OBJECTION 6 - typically about access and format\]**

_Example: "How do I access the books? Do I get PDFs? Can I read on my phone?"_

\[ANSWER - be specific about delivery\]

**\[OBJECTION 7 - typically about sharing\]**

_Example: "Can I share this with my spouse / small group?"_

\[ANSWER\]

**\[OBJECTION 8 - typically about overlap with prior reading\]**

_Example: "What if I've already read some of these books?"_

\[ANSWER\]

_Example: "If you've read this far, you're probably close to a decision. Here's what I want you to know before you make it."_

**SECTION 12 - THE FINAL CLOSE AND CTA**

**Purpose:** Land emotional weight one last time and invite the decision. **Methods:** 4 (Transformation Bridge crossed), 10 (Kingdom Urgency - full deployment), 7 (StoryBrand call to action) **Length:** 200-400 words.

**\[CLOSING SUBHEAD - names the choice\]**

_Example structures:_

- _"Two Paths From Here"_
- _"What \[TIME PERIOD\] From Now Looks Like"_
- _"The Decision in Front of You"_

\[PARAGRAPH 1 - paint the path of staying where the reader is\]

_Structure: "If you close this page and nothing changes, \[SPECIFIC OUTCOME of continued inaction\]. The \[PAIN\] doesn't get smaller - it just keeps shaping you while you carry it alone."_

\[PARAGRAPH 2 - paint the path of saying yes\]

_Structure: "If you say yes today, \[N\] days from now you'll \[SPECIFIC OUTCOME\]. \[TIMEFRAME\] from now you'll \[DEEPER OUTCOME\]. _"\[CLOSING VERSE - Hebrews 3:15, 2 Corinthians 6:2, James 1:22, or similar\]"_ - \[REFERENCE\]

**Still Deciding?**

**PRICE TIER ADJUSTMENTS**

The skeleton above produces a mid-ticket page (\$77-\$197). For other tiers, adjust as follows.

**Low-ticket (\$7-\$67)**

- Collapse Sections 3, 5, 8, 11 (use only 4-6 FAQ entries)
- Shorten Section 7 (less stacking needed)
- Skip Section 13
- Total page length target: 1,500-2,500 words

**High-ticket (\$297+)**

- Expand Section 2 (full origin story, longer testimony)
- Expand Section 4 (deeper Big Idea exploration, multiple supporting scriptures)
- Add a "Methodology" section between 4 and 5 explaining the system in depth
- Expand Section 7 (more components, deeper descriptions)
- Expand Section 8 (7-15 testimonials)
- Expand Section 11 (10-15 FAQs)
- Add payment plan prominently in Section 9
- Total page length target: 6,000-10,000 words

**Upsell pages (\$297 library, \$497 coaching, etc.)**

- Use templates/upsell-page-skeleton.md instead - different psychology entirely
- Compress to Sections 1, 4 (frame shift), 6, 7, 10, 12
- Skip Sections 3, 5, 11
- Total page length target: 1,200-2,500 words

**CLAUDE'S WORKFLOW USING THIS SKELETON**

- **Run intake** (templates/intake-questionnaire.md) - gather all variables.
- **Load foundation references** - philosophy.md, voice-guide.md, awareness-stages.md.
- **Select method emphasis** - based on price tier and intake answers, determine which sections get expanded and which get compressed.
- **Fill the skeleton** - replace every \[BRACKET\] with offer-specific content. Pull from method files (method-01 through method-10) for section-specific writing guidance.
- **Generate headlines** - use templates/headline-generator.md to produce 20 headline options, pick top 3, recommend one with reasoning.
- **Calibrate voice** - apply templates/voice-calibration.md to match the author's tone, theology, and denomination.
- **Self-audit** - check the draft against references/conversion-killers.md before delivery.
- **Deliver** - output the finished page with a brief annotation showing which methods were used in which sections, so the user can request adjustments intelligently.

**THE UNDERLYING PRINCIPLE**

This skeleton is a structure, not a script. The goal is not to produce identical pages for every offer - it is to ensure that every page hits the necessary psychological, emotional, and decisional beats in the right order, with the right methods deployed at the right moments.

When in doubt about a specific section, ask: _what does this section need to do for the reader's heart, mind, and decision?_ The bracketed structure is there to keep Claude from forgetting any of those jobs. The actual writing is where the page becomes specific, alive, and converting.
