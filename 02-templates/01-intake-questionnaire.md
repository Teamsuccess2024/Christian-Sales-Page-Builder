**Questions to Ask Before Writing**

**Purpose of the Intake**

Before Claude writes a single line of sales copy, the intake phase gathers the specific inputs that determine whether the resulting page will convert or fall flat. A Christian sales page lives or dies on specificity - the wrong reader avatar, a vague pain point, or a missing transformation will undermine even excellent copy.

The questions below are organized into seven sections. Claude should run through them in order, but with adaptive intelligence: if the user has already provided some answers in earlier conversation or in memory context, Claude should not re-ask those questions. The goal is to fill in genuine gaps, not to interrogate the user.

If the user has provided most context already, Claude can compress the intake into a short confirmation: _"Before I draft, let me confirm a few details..."_ and ask only the questions still open.

For users who want to move fast, Claude can offer two paths at the start: a full intake (15-20 questions) or a minimum-viable intake (the five questions marked **ESSENTIAL** below). The full intake produces a more precisely calibrated page. The minimum intake is enough to draft something serviceable.

**Section 1 - The Offer Itself**

These questions establish what is actually being sold. Without clarity here, every downstream choice is guesswork.

**1.1 - ESSENTIAL** - What is the offer? Describe it in one or two sentences as you'd explain it to a friend over coffee.

**1.2** - What format is the offer delivered in? (HTML pages on a site, downloadable PDFs, video modules, audio recordings, physical books, a membership area, live cohort access, a combination?)

**1.3** - How does the buyer access it after purchase? Walk through the experience step by step.

**1.4** - How many components are inside the offer? List each one by title if possible. (For a bundle: how many books? For a course: how many modules? For a membership: what's included?)

**1.5** - Is there anything physical involved, or is it fully digital?

**1.6** - Is the content already live and accessible, or is some of it still being created? If still being created, what's the timeline and how does the buyer get notified?

**1.7** - Are any of these components currently available for free elsewhere (on your site, on YouTube, in a free email sequence)? If yes, what's the buyer paying for that's different - curation, format, order, bundling, convenience?

**Section 2 - Pricing and Funnel Structure**

These questions establish what tier the offer sits in and how it fits inside the broader funnel.

**2.1 - ESSENTIAL** - What is the price of this specific offer?

**2.2** - Is this the primary offer, an upsell, a downsell, or a tripwire? Walk me through where this page sits in the full funnel.

**2.3** - What comes before this offer in the funnel? (Free lead magnet, email opt-in, tripwire purchase, etc.)

**2.4** - What comes after this offer in the funnel? (Upsells, downsells, post-purchase emails, retargeting.)

**2.5** - Are payment plans available? If yes, what are the options? (2-pay, 3-pay, 4-pay, etc.)

**2.6** - Is there a real, honest discount or founding-member pricing in play? Or is this the stable, evergreen price?

**2.7** - Is there a money-back guarantee? If yes, what's the window (30 days, 60 days, etc.) and what are the conditions?

**Section 3 - The Reader (Buyer Avatar)**

These questions establish exactly who the page is written for. Vague avatars produce vague copy.

**3.1 - ESSENTIAL** - Describe the ideal buyer in detail. Who specifically is this for? (Age range, life stage, gender if relevant, current spiritual season, family situation, work context.)

**3.2** - What is the buyer's current pain point or aspiration in their own words? Use language the buyer would actually use, not marketing language.

**3.3** - What has the buyer already tried to solve this? What hasn't worked, and why?

**3.4** - Where is the buyer on the awareness spectrum? (Unaware they have this problem, problem-aware but skeptical of solutions, solution-aware and comparing options, product-aware and ready to buy, or most aware and just needing a nudge.)

**3.5** - What denomination range is this for? (Broadly evangelical / non-denominational, charismatic / Pentecostal, Reformed / Calvinist, Catholic, mainline Protestant, mixed audience, or denomination-specific?)

**3.6** - What level of biblical literacy does the buyer have? (New believer learning the basics, lifelong churchgoer with foundational knowledge, theologically literate and reading commentaries, somewhere in between.)

**3.7** - What's the buyer's relationship with Christian media currently? (Eager and trusting, burned and skeptical, somewhere in between.) This shapes how much trust-building the page needs to do.

**3.8** - What is the buyer afraid of regarding this purchase? What objections are silently running through their head?

**Section 4 - The Specific Pain or Aspiration**

These questions go deeper into the problem the offer solves. The more specific the pain, the more specific the copy can be.

**4.1 - ESSENTIAL** - What is the specific pain, struggle, or aspiration this offer addresses? Be as concrete as possible - not "suffering" but "suffering you didn't choose, where God feels silent and prayer feels like talking to the ceiling."

**4.2** - What is the cost of leaving this pain unaddressed? What happens to the reader's faith, relationships, identity, or daily life if they continue without help?

**4.3** - What does the reader currently believe about their pain that may not be biblically accurate? (Examples: pain is punishment, suffering means God is distant, my struggle is unique and unfixable.)

**4.4** - What is the reader silently afraid is true about themselves? (Examples: my faith isn't strong enough, I'm being punished, God has given up on me.)

**4.5** - When the reader is alone at night, what is the thought that loops in their head about this pain?

**4.6** - What has the reader already tried? (Books that didn't help, pastors who gave platitudes, prayer that felt unanswered, counseling, journaling, etc.)

**Section 5 - The Author and the Story**

These questions establish the voice, authority, and testimony behind the page. Christian buyers buy from people, not from brands.

**5.1 - ESSENTIAL** - What is the author's personal testimony related to this offer? Has the author walked through this pain themselves? Tell me the story.

**5.2** - What credentials, life experience, ministry context, or platform does the author bring? (Previous books, ministry leadership, counseling background, speaking history, audience size, etc.)

**5.3** - What is the author's voice like? (Warm and pastoral, direct and challenging, gentle and meditative, prophetic and bold, friendly and conversational, scholarly and precise, or some combination.) If unsure, provide a sample of the author's writing - a paragraph from one of their books, a social post, or a recent email.

**5.4** - What is the author's theological positioning? (Are there doctrinal positions the author is known for? Are there positions the author wants to avoid taking on this page?)

**5.5** - How does the author want to be perceived by the reader? (As a fellow traveler, as a trusted teacher, as a guide, as a pastor, as an author, as a friend?)

**5.6** - Are there influences or thinkers the author wants the page to echo? (Specific authors, teachers, or traditions whose voice the page should resemble.)

**Section 6 - Scripture and Theological Anchors**

These questions establish the biblical foundation of the offer. Specific verses make the page convert; vague spirituality does not.

**6.1** - What are the 3-5 anchor scriptures for this offer? Verses that the offer's methodology rests on, or that capture the transformation it delivers.

**6.2** - Is there a biblical figure whose story parallels the buyer's journey? (Joseph, Job, Hannah, Naomi, Paul, David, Peter, etc.) The right figure becomes a powerful narrative anchor.

**6.3** - Is there a single "Big Idea" - a contrarian or fresh biblical truth - that reframes the reader's situation? If you had to name the page's central idea in one sentence, what would it be?

**6.4** - What Bible translation does the author prefer? (NIV is the default for this skill, but ESV, NLT, KJV, or others may be appropriate depending on audience.)

**6.5** - Are there any theological positions the page must avoid taking (to keep the audience broad), or any positions the page must clearly take (because the audience expects it)?

**Section 7 - The Transformation**

These questions establish what the buyer will look like, feel like, and live like after using the offer. The transformation is what the buyer is actually paying for.

**7.1 - ESSENTIAL** - What is the specific transformation this offer delivers? Describe the buyer's "after" state in concrete terms - not "they'll feel better" but "they'll have a biblical framework for their suffering, scriptures memorized for their hardest moments, and the quiet authority of someone who has met God in the valley."

**7.2** - What does the buyer's daily life look like after they've fully engaged with the offer?

**7.3** - How does the buyer's prayer life, scripture intake, or spiritual practice change?

**7.4** - How does the buyer's relationship with their pain, their identity, or their season change?

**7.5** - What can the buyer now do for others that they couldn't do before? (Christian transformation usually flows outward - the buyer becomes a resource to their family, small group, or community.)

**7.6** - Are there past buyers who can serve as testimonials for this transformation? If yes, can you share 3-10 of them with names, locations, and specific outcomes?

**7.7** - If no testimonials exist yet, are there testimonials about the author's previous work, about the source material, or about the methodology that could be repurposed honestly?

**Section 8 - Practical Production Details**

These questions establish the practical elements needed to ship the page.

**8.1** - Do book covers, product mockups, or visual assets exist for the offer? If not, will they be created before launch?

**8.2** - Is there an existing brand style guide, color palette, or font system the page should match?

**8.3** - Where will the page live? (Hosted on Team Success Network, on a funnel platform like ClickFunnels, on a standalone landing page builder, etc.)

**8.4** - What's the timeline for launch?

**8.5** - Will the page need a mobile-optimized version specifically? (Most Christian buyers read on phone - the answer is almost always yes.)

**8.6** - Are there bonuses or fast-action incentives planned? If yes, what are they and what's their stated value?

**8.7** - Is there anything else about this offer, this audience, or this season that would help me write a page that converts well?

**How Claude Handles the Intake**

Claude runs the intake adaptively, not robotically. Specifically:

**If the user has provided extensive context already** (through memory, prior conversation, or uploaded materials), Claude reviews what's known and asks only the genuinely open questions. Format: _"Based on what you've shared, I have the offer, the price point, the audience, and the testimony. Let me ask three things I still need..."_

**If the user wants to move fast**, Claude offers the minimum-viable intake - the five ESSENTIAL questions only - and proceeds with reasonable assumptions for the rest. Format: _"We can do a full intake (about 20 questions, sharper page) or a minimum intake (5 questions, faster). Which do you prefer?"_

**If the user provides ambiguous answers**, Claude follows up with one clarifying question rather than accepting vague input. Vague answers produce vague pages.

**If the user provides answers that conflict** (e.g., theological positioning that doesn't match the stated audience), Claude flags the conflict gently and asks which direction to take.

**Claude never asks more than 3-5 questions at a time.** Long question lists feel like interrogation. Group questions into small batches, let the user answer, then continue.

**Claude saves intake answers in the conversation context** so they can be referenced and revised throughout the drafting process. If the user changes an answer mid-draft, Claude updates the affected sections accordingly.

**Minimum-Viable Intake (The Five Essentials)**

For users who want to move fast, these five questions are the absolute minimum needed to produce a serviceable page:

- **What is the offer?** (One or two sentences.)
- **What is the price?** (Including any payment plans.)
- **Who is the ideal buyer?** (Specific avatar.)
- **What is the specific pain or aspiration?** (Concrete, not vague.)
- **What is the transformation?** (Concrete, not vague.)

With these five answers, Claude can draft a competent page using reasonable defaults for everything else. The page won't be as sharp as one built on the full intake, but it will be functional and structurally sound.

**The Underlying Principle**

The intake is not a formality. Every question above maps to a specific decision Claude will make while writing: which methods to deploy, which scriptures to anchor with, which tone to calibrate to, which objections to crush in the FAQ, which transformation language to use in the close.

A page built on a thorough intake reads as if it was written for one specific person sitting in one specific season of life. A page built on guesses reads as generic Christian marketing. The difference shows up in conversion rates immediately.

When in doubt, ask one more question. The five extra minutes of intake will save hours of revision later.
