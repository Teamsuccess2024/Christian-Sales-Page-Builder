**Upsell Page Structure - Library and Membership Offers**

**Why Upsells Need a Different Skeleton**

A primary sales page and an upsell page do fundamentally different jobs, and treating them the same way is one of the most common reasons upsells fail.

The primary page convinces a stranger to buy. It builds awareness, dismantles resistance, proves the offer, and earns the first yes. The buyer arrives uncertain and leaves committed.

The upsell page does something different. The buyer has already said yes. They've already entered their card. They already trust the seller enough to have transacted. The upsell is not persuading a stranger - it is offering a deeper relationship to someone who has just stepped through the door.

This requires a complete psychological frame shift. The primary offer solves _today's pain_. The upsell offers _lifetime resourcing_. The primary offer is about the immediate problem. The upsell is about every season the buyer will walk through for the rest of their life.

Get the frame shift wrong and the upsell feels like a bait-and-switch. The buyer who just trustingly purchased feels ambushed, and refund requests on the original purchase spike. Get the frame shift right and the upsell feels like the natural next step - a deeper invitation to someone who has already chosen the path.

This skeleton produces a \$197-\$497 library or membership upsell. For coaching or live-program upsells, the structure shifts again (covered briefly at the end).

**THE FRAME SHIFT IN ONE SENTENCE**

The buyer just purchased the _solution to today's pain_. The upsell is not more pain-solving. It is _the complete spiritual library for every season they'll walk through for the rest of their life._

Every section of this skeleton reinforces that frame shift. If a section drifts back into solving today's pain, it needs to be rewritten.

**SECTION 1 - THE TRANSITION ACKNOWLEDGMENT**

**Purpose:** Acknowledge the buyer's first yes warmly. Make them feel welcomed, not flipped into a new pitch. **Methods:** 1 (Testimony - author's voice, present), 4 (Transformation Bridge - affirming the bridge already crossed) **Length:** 75-150 words.

**Wait - Before You Go to \[PRIMARY OFFER\] Access...**

\[OPENING - warmly acknowledge what just happened\]

_Example: "You just said yes to \[PRIMARY OFFER NAME\], and I'm grateful. In the next 60 seconds you'll have full access. But before you get there, I want to make you one offer - and only one - that most Christians who say yes to \[PRIMARY OFFER\] end up wishing they'd taken."_

\[BRIDGE LINE INTO THE FRAME SHIFT\]

_Example: "It's not more of what you just bought. It's something different - and it's why this page exists."_

**SECTION 2 - THE FRAME SHIFT**

**Purpose:** Explicitly shift the buyer's mental frame from "I solved today's pain" to "I want lifetime resourcing for every season ahead." **Methods:** 2 (Big Idea - reframed for upsell), 4 (Transformation Bridge - expanded to lifetime scope) **Length:** 250-400 words.

**\[SUBHEAD - names the frame shift\]**

_Example structures:_

- _"\[PRIMARY OFFER\] Was for Today. This Is for Every Season Ahead."_
- _"You Just Got the Resource for \[CURRENT PAIN\]. Here's the Resource for the Next \[N\] Seasons of Your Life."_
- _"What If You Never Had to Look for the Right Christian Book Again?"_

\[PARAGRAPH 1 - name what the primary offer is and what it isn't\]

_Structure: "\[PRIMARY OFFER NAME\] does one thing well - it walks you through \[SPECIFIC PAIN\]. And that's enough for today. But here's what I know about following Jesus: \[CURRENT PAIN\] isn't the last season you'll walk through. There will be \[LIST 2-3 OTHER LIFE SEASONS - marriage strain, parenting questions, vocational confusion, deconstruction seasons, grief, joy, calling shifts, etc.\]."_

\[PARAGRAPH 2 - paint the lifetime resourcing vision\]

_Structure: "For each of those seasons, there's a book that will meet you. There's a teacher who has walked it. There's scripture that speaks directly to it. The question is whether you'll have those books on hand when you need them - or whether you'll be searching at midnight for the right resource while the pain compounds."_

\[PARAGRAPH 3 - introduce the upsell as the answer to that question\]

_Structure: "That's what \[UPSELL OFFER NAME\] exists for. It's not a course. It's not a bundle. It's \[N\]+ Christian books across \[N\] topics - the complete library I've curated for \[AUTHOR'S MINISTRY / TEAM SUCCESS NETWORK\] - yours for life."_

\[BRIDGE INTO SECTION 3\]

_Example: "Here's exactly what's inside, and why I'm offering it at this price only on this page."_

**SECTION 3 - THE LIBRARY REVEAL**

**Purpose:** Show the scope of the library in a way that conveys "comprehensive" without overwhelming. **Methods:** 5 (Curated Library Frame - full deployment), 6 (Value Stack - at the library scale) **Length:** 300-500 words.

**What's Inside \[UPSELL OFFER NAME\]**

\[OPENING LINE - name the scope plainly\]

_Example: "\[N\]+ books. \[N\] topic categories. Every season of the Christian walk addressed."_

\[VISUAL: Grid or stack showing the library at scale - book covers tiled, category badges, or a clean infographic of topic categories\]

\[CATEGORY LIST - show the breadth without listing every book\]

_Format the categories as a clean, scannable list. Example structure:_

- **\[CATEGORY 1\]** - \[N\] books on \[TOPIC DESCRIPTION\]
- **\[CATEGORY 2\]** - \[N\] books on \[TOPIC DESCRIPTION\]
- **\[CATEGORY 3\]** - \[N\] books on \[TOPIC DESCRIPTION\]
- **\[CATEGORY 4\]** - \[N\] books on \[TOPIC DESCRIPTION\]

_\[Continue for all 30 or so categories. The visual weight of the list itself communicates "comprehensive."\]_

\[PARAGRAPH - narrate the library's purpose\]

_Structure: "Every book is anchored in scripture. Every topic represents a real season Christians walk through. And every book is written in plain language for the believer who wants depth without academic friction. You don't need to read all \[N\]+ books - you need to know they're there when the season comes."_

\[OPTIONAL - author's note on curation\]

_Example: "I've spent \[N\] years building this library, one book at a time. Every book made the cut for the same reason: it would have helped me in a specific season of my walk with Jesus. That's the standard. If it would have helped me, it's in the library. If it wouldn't have, it isn't."_

\[BRIDGE INTO SECTION 4\]

_Example: "Now here's why this is on this page instead of available anywhere else."_

**SECTION 4 - THE UPSELL-SPECIFIC POSITIONING**

**Purpose:** Explain why this offer is available here and now, at this price, and not elsewhere. Justify the upsell without manufactured urgency. **Methods:** 10 (Risk Reversal + honest urgency) **Length:** 200-300 words.

**Why This Offer Lives Here**

\[PARAGRAPH 1 - the honest reason this upsell exists on this page\]

_Structure: "Most Christians never find this library. They land on the \[TEAM SUCCESS NETWORK\] site, browse a few books, and leave. The Christians who actually use this library are the ones who first bought \[PRIMARY OFFER NAME\] - because that means they already trust the work. So this is the one page where I make this offer, at this price, to the readers who've already chosen the path."_

\[PARAGRAPH 2 - what the buyer gets and what makes it different\]

_Structure: "Outside this page, the books are available free on the site, one at a time, without curation, without download, without the structured topic groupings, and without the \[BONUS - if applicable\]. On this page, you get \[SPECIFIC LIBRARY ACCESS BENEFITS\]: the full library bundled, organized, and yours for life."_

\[PARAGRAPH 3 - honest framing of the price tier\]

_Structure: "The price reflects what this is: a one-time investment in a resource library that will meet you in every season for the rest of your life. Not a monthly subscription. Not a recurring charge. One decision, lifetime access."_

\[BRIDGE INTO SECTION 5\]

_Example: "Here's exactly what's included."_

**SECTION 5 - THE COMPRESSED VALUE STACK**

**Purpose:** Build value at the library scale, not at the individual-book scale. **Methods:** 6 (Value Stack with Stewardship Framing - adapted for library scale) **Length:** 300-500 words.

**Your \[UPSELL OFFER NAME\] Includes**

**Full Library Access - \[N\]+ Books**

**What's included:** Every book currently in the \[TEAM SUCCESS NETWORK\] library, plus every book added in the future. Lifetime access, no recurring charges.

**Value:** \$\[STACKED VALUE - calculate honestly: N books × realistic per-book value\]

**\[N\] Curated Topic Collections**

**What's included:** The library organized into \[N\] topical groupings - so you find the right book for the right season without searching.

**Value:** \$\[VALUE\]

**\[BONUS 1, IF APPLICABLE\]**

_Examples: Quarterly new-book additions, early access to upcoming releases, a personal-use license to share with spouse / household, a member-only reading guide._

**Why it's included:** \[REASON\]

**Value:** \$\[VALUE\]

**\[BONUS 2, IF APPLICABLE\]**

\[Same structure\]

**Total Value: \$\[STACKED TOTAL\]**

\[STEWARDSHIP FRAMING LINE - adapted for lifetime resourcing\]

_Pick one:_

- _"That's less than \$\[PER-BOOK MATH\] per book over the \[N\] books currently in the library - before counting every book added in the future."_
- _"For one-time investment, you're getting what would cost \$\[REALISTIC COMPARISON\] if purchased separately at a Christian bookstore over the years."_
- _"A single Christian conference weekend costs more. This is yours for life."_

**SECTION 6 - THE STEWARDSHIP FRAME**

**Purpose:** Reframe the price as wise stewardship of long-term resourcing, not impulse expense. **Methods:** 6 (Stewardship Framing), 10 (Risk Reversal) **Length:** 150-250 words.

**Your \[UPSELL OFFER NAME\] Investment**

\[REVEAL THE PRICE\]

_Example: "Your price today, only on this page:_ **_\$\[UPSELL PRICE\]_**_"_

\[STEWARDSHIP FRAMING PARAGRAPH\]

_Structure: "If you bought \[N\] Christian books at \$\[REALISTIC RETAIL\] each over the next ten years, you'd spend \$\[STACKED COMPARISON\]. If you sat with a Christian counselor for \[N\] sessions to talk through what these books address, you'd spend \$\[COMPARISON\]. The \[UPSELL PRICE\] today is what wise stewardship looks like - paying once for what serves you, your family, and the people you walk alongside for the rest of your life."_

\[PAYMENT PLAN, IF OFFERED\]

_Example: "Or \[N\] payments of \$\[AMOUNT\], billed monthly. Same access, same library, spread over \[N\] months for households that need it."_

**\[CTA BUTTON - primary placement\]**

_CTA copy options:_

- _"Add the Full Library - \$\[PRICE\]"_
- _"Yes, Add Lifetime Access for \$\[PRICE\]"_
- _"Upgrade to the Complete Library"_

\[UNDER-BUTTON TRUST LINE\]

_Example: "One-time payment. Lifetime access. 30-day guarantee."_

**SECTION 7 - THE GUARANTEE (CONDENSED)**

**Purpose:** Remove the risk of the upsell decision specifically. **Methods:** 10 (Risk Reversal) **Length:** 75-150 words.

**The Same Guarantee, Extended**

\[GUARANTEE STATEMENT\]

_Structure: "The \[N\]-day guarantee on \[PRIMARY OFFER NAME\] applies to \[UPSELL OFFER NAME\] too. If the library doesn't meet you where you are, email \[EMAIL\] within \[N\] days and I'll refund the upgrade - and you keep \[PRIMARY OFFER NAME\] either way. No risk on top of the yes you already gave."_

\[ONE-LINE PASTORAL CLOSE TO GUARANTEE\]

_Example: "I'd rather lose the upgrade than have you feel pressured into something that doesn't serve you."_

**SECTION 8 - THE COMPRESSED CLOSE AND DECLINE OPTION**

**Purpose:** Invite the yes one final time. Make the decline option visible and gracious. **Methods:** 4 (Transformation Bridge - final), 7 (StoryBrand - final call to action), 10 (honest Kingdom urgency) **Length:** 200-350 words.

**Two Paths From Here**

\[PARAGRAPH 1 - paint the decline path honestly, without guilt\]

_Structure: "You can click 'No thanks' below and go straight to \[PRIMARY OFFER NAME\] access. That's a fully legitimate choice. You'll get exactly what you bought, the \[N\]-day guarantee still applies, and we're good."_

\[PARAGRAPH 2 - paint the yes path\]

_Structure: "Or you can add \[UPSELL OFFER NAME\] right now and walk into not just \[PRIMARY OFFER NAME\] access, but the full library - \[N\]+ books across every season you'll walk through, organized by topic, yours for life. The upgrade price you see here is the only place I offer it. After today, the library is still accessible book-by-book on the site, but this bundled, lifetime version exists only on this page."_

\[OPTIONAL - closing scripture for the decision, used carefully\]

_"\[CLOSING VERSE - used only if it fits naturally and doesn't pressure\]"_ - \[REFERENCE\]

\[FINAL CTA SECTION\]

**\[CTA BUTTON - primary yes - visually prominent\]**

_Example: "Yes, Add the Full Library for \$\[PRICE\]"_

\[DECLINE OPTION - smaller, less prominent, but visible\]

_Example link text: "No thanks, just give me \[PRIMARY OFFER NAME\] access"_

_Place the decline option as a small link below the main CTA, not a competing button. The yes should visually dominate, but the no should be obvious and graceful._

\[OPTIONAL SIGNATURE LINE\]

_Example:_ _"Walking this with you,"_ _\[AUTHOR NAME\]_

**WHAT THIS SKELETON DELIBERATELY OMITS**

A primary sales page does many things this upsell page does not. Understanding what's missing - and why - is part of getting the frame shift right.

**No empathy / problem opening.** The buyer is past that. They've already named their pain and bought the solution. Re-running the empathy section makes them feel re-pitched.

**No problem-deepening section.** Same reason. The pain is acknowledged. The bridge is being crossed. The upsell is about what's on the other side, not about the river again.

**No belief breaking.** The buyer has already shown they don't hold the disqualifying beliefs - they bought. Belief-breaking on an upsell page reads as condescending.

**No full FAQ section.** A few inline objection-handlers can appear in the close, but a 12-question FAQ on an upsell page is overkill and slows the decision.

**No full social proof section.** A single testimonial about the library (if one exists) can appear after the value stack. A 7-testimonial section breaks the upsell tempo.

**No theatrical urgency.** The honest urgency here is that this price and this bundled format exists only on this page. That's enough. No countdown timers, no fake scarcity, no "this offer expires when you leave this page" pressure language even if technically true.

**UPSELL-SPECIFIC CONVERSION KILLERS**

In addition to everything in references/conversion-killers.md, upsells have their own failure modes.

**The flip into pressure voice.** The buyer just trustingly purchased. If the upsell voice suddenly becomes louder, more urgent, more sales-heavy than the primary page, the buyer feels manipulated. Voice continuity matters more on upsells than anywhere else in the funnel.

**The "secret" upsell-only pricing trick.** Claiming the upsell price is only available "on this page, right now, never again" when it's actually offered regularly or accessible elsewhere. Christian buyers research. If they discover the price was a manipulation, they refund the primary purchase too.

**The bait-and-switch.** Selling primary offer X, then upselling something fundamentally different from what the buyer signed up for. The upsell needs to feel like a natural deeper step in the same path, not a pivot to a different product line.

**The hidden decline button.** Making "no thanks" so small, so faint, or so confusingly worded that buyers can't easily opt out. This generates refunds on the primary purchase and damages long-term reputation.

**Too many upsells in a chain.** Adding a second upsell, a downsell, a bump, and a confirmation upsell turns the post-purchase experience into a gauntlet. One upsell per primary purchase is usually right for Christian audiences. Maybe two if both are genuinely well-positioned.

**PRICE TIER ADJUSTMENTS FOR UPSELLS**

The skeleton above produces a library or membership upsell at \$197-\$497. For other upsell tiers:

**Lower-tier upsells (\$47-\$97 order bump or low-ticket upsell)**

- Compress the skeleton to Sections 1, 2 (shortened), 3 (shortened), 6, 7, 8
- Total length: 600-1,200 words
- Lighter stewardship framing - at this price, the buyer is making a small additional decision

**Coaching or live-program upsells (\$497+)**

- Use the high-ticket primary skeleton (full-sales-page-skeleton.md) instead, lightly compressed
- Coaching upsells need more trust-building because they involve access to a person, not just access to content
- Add a Section 4.5 - "What This Coaching Includes" - with specifics on session count, format, duration, and access
- Expand Section 5 with detailed program structure
- Add an extended FAQ section addressing coaching-specific concerns (commitment, scheduling, refunds mid-program)
- Total length: 3,000-5,000 words

**Recurring membership upsells (monthly or annual)**

- Modify Section 6 stewardship framing to address the recurring nature honestly
- Add explicit cancellation policy in Section 7
- Use annual pricing where possible, with month-equivalent math shown
- Be especially careful about lifetime-versus-recurring confusion - Christian buyers strongly prefer one-time payments over subscriptions

**CLAUDE'S WORKFLOW USING THIS SKELETON**

- **Confirm this is actually an upsell scenario.** If the buyer hasn't already purchased something, this is the wrong skeleton - use full-sales-page-skeleton.md instead.
- **Pull the primary offer context.** What did the buyer just purchase? At what price? What pain did it address? The upsell positioning depends entirely on what the buyer already said yes to.
- **Identify the frame shift specifically.** What is the primary offer's narrow scope, and what is the upsell's broader scope? Write the frame shift sentence first, before any other copy. Every section flows from that one sentence.
- **Fill the skeleton.** Replace every \[BRACKET\] with offer-specific content. Pull voice continuity from the primary sales page - the buyer should feel the same voice continuing, not a new pitch beginning.
- **Audit specifically against upsell conversion killers.** Beyond the standard conversion-killer audit, check the voice continuity, the decline-button visibility, and the absence of pressure-flip language.
- **Deliver.** Output the finished upsell page with annotation showing the frame shift sentence, the methods deployed, and any voice-continuity decisions worth confirming with the user.

**THE UNDERLYING PRINCIPLE**

A well-designed upsell honors the trust the buyer just extended. The buyer said yes once. The upsell asks: _would you like to go deeper into the same path, or are you set with what you already chose?_ Both answers are gracious. Both answers leave the buyer feeling respected.

The upsell page that converts highest is not the one with the most clever frame or the loudest pitch. It is the one where the buyer who says yes feels welcomed deeper into the journey, and the buyer who says no feels just as warmly sent on their way with what they already bought.

When in doubt, write the upsell page as if the buyer is sitting across from you at a table, having just paid for a meal, and you're offering them dessert. The yes is welcomed. The no is fine. Either way, they leave fed.
